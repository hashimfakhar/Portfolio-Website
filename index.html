About the Artist

Hash is a contemporary fine artist based in Portland, Oregon, specializing in oil and acrylic paintings that explore the emotional resonance of color and texture. Her work bridges the gap between abstract expressionism and impressionistic landscapes, creating pieces that invite viewers to find their own meaning within the layers of paint.

With over 15 years of dedicated practice, Hash has developed a distinctive style characterized by bold brushwork, rich color palettes, and an intuitive approach to composition. Her paintings have been exhibited in galleries across the Pacific Northwest and are held in private collections worldwide.

The Creative Process

From blank canvas to finished masterpiece

01

Inspiration & Sketching

Every painting begins with observation — a fleeting moment of light, an emotional response to nature, or a color combination that demands exploration. Initial sketches capture the essence before paint ever touches canvas.

02

Underpainting & Layering

The foundation is laid with thin washes of color, establishing value and temperature. For oils, this might be a traditional imprimatura; for acrylics, a bold colored ground that peeks through subsequent layers.

03

Building Texture

Using palette knives, brushes, and unconventional tools, texture is built up to create depth and visual interest. Each layer is allowed to dry (or partially dry) before the next is applied, creating complex interactions of color.

04

Refinement & Varnishing

The final stage involves careful refinement of focal points, adjustment of values, and the application of a protective varnish. For oil paintings, this comes after months of curing; acrylics can be varnished within days.
